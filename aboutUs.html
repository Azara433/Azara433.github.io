ABOUT REGION 3

Overview of the Region

Region Three, officially known as Essequibo Islands - West Demerara is a region rich in history, culture, and breathtaking landscapes. Home to thriving agricultural communities, scenic waterways, and historic Dutch ruins, this region is a true gem of Guyana. Whether you're exploring its peaceful islands or discovering its colonial past, Region Three offers a unique blend of adventure and tradition.

Geography of the Land

The Essequibo Islands - West Demerara extends from the East bank of the Essequibo River to the West Bank of the Demerara River. The Region has a number of islands at the mouth of the Essequibo River and has an area of 1460 square miles (3770 square kilometers). It comprises of mostly of the Coastal Plain, the Hilly Sand and Clay Region and small portions of the Hinterland Forest Region.

Official Flag of Region 3

The colours for this flag are green to represent the rich agricultural value, black to represent the endurance of the people, and white to depict the water resources. The Symbol is the M.V. Torani.

Agriculture: The Heart of the Region

Agriculture is the backbone of Region Three’s economy, with rice, sugar cane, and coconuts as the main crops. The Boerasirie Extension Project plays a key role in irrigation, allowing farmers to sustain their crops even in the driest seasons. The region is also home to cattle farms, contributing to Guyana’s dairy and beef production.
